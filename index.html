FICHA TECNICA

Titulo The effect of preservice teachers’ ICT integration self-efficacy beliefs on their ICT competencies: The mediating role of online self-regulated learning strategies
Autores Zhuo Zhang , Yukiko Maeda , Timothy Newby , Zui Cheng , Qian Xu
Resumen This study explored how preservice teachers' (PSTs) perceived self-efficacy beliefs for integrating information and communication technology (ICT) relate to their actual acquisition of ICT competencies directly and indirectly through their perceived use of online self-regulated learning (SRL) strategies. Participants included 204 PSTs enrolled in an undergraduate introductory educational technology course. A structural model was built to test the causal relations among ICT integration self-efficacy, combined SRL strategies, and ICT competencies. Additional analyses were conducted with six new structural models for testing each type of SRL strategy: goal setting; environmental structuring; task strategies; time management; help-seeking; self-evaluation. Results indicated that PSTs’ perceived ICT integration self-efficacy did not directly affect their ICT competencies but was mediated by their perceived use of goal-setting strategies. Also, ICT integration self-efficacy had a positive moderate direct effect on the combined SRL strategies and all single types of SRL strategies, except self-evaluative strategies. The report concludes with a discussion of implications and limitations.
Palabras clave Information and communication technology (ICT), Technology integration, Self-efficacy, Self-regulated learning, Structuring equation modeling

PREGUNTAS IA

What is the objective of the article?

The objective of the article is to investigate the relationship between preservice teachers' self-efficacy beliefs for integrating ICT (Information and Communication Technologies) and their actual acquisition of ICT competencies. The study aims to explore how preservice teachers' perceived self-efficacy in ICT integration influences their development of ICT competencies through the use of online self-regulated learning strategies.

What methodology did you use to develop the article?

The methodology used in developing the article involved the following steps: Participants: The study included 204 preservice teachers enrolled in an undergraduate introductory educational technology course. Data Collection: Data on self-efficacy beliefs, self-regulated learning strategies, and ICT competencies were collected through self-reporting by the participants. Structural Model: A structural model was built to test the causal relations among ICT integration self-efficacy, combined self-regulated learning strategies, and ICT competencies. Analysis: Additional analyses were conducted with six new structural models to test each type of self-regulated learning strategy individually. Results Interpretation: The results indicated the direct and indirect effects of self-efficacy beliefs on ICT competencies through self-regulated learning strategies. Discussion: The study concluded with a discussion of the implications and limitations of the findings. This methodology allowed the researchers to examine the complex relationship between preservice teachers' self-efficacy beliefs, self-regulated learning strategies, and ICT competencies in the context of online learning.

What was the reason for creating this article?

The article was created to address a research gap in the field of educational technology regarding the relationship between preservice teachers' self-efficacy beliefs for integrating ICT and their actual acquisition of ICT competencies. The researchers aimed to investigate how these self-efficacy beliefs influence the development of ICT competencies through the use of online self-regulated learning strategies. By exploring this relationship, the study sought to provide insights that could inform teacher education programs and interventions aimed at enhancing preservice teachers' skills in integrating technology effectively in the classroom.

What obstacles did writing the article have?

The obstacles encountered in writing the article may include: Data Limitations: The reliance on self-reporting for data collection could introduce biases and inaccuracies due to participants' personal beliefs and external influences. Measurement Challenges: The outcomes of the study may have been affected by how the researchers measured ICT competencies, highlighting the need for further research on measuring competencies with actual performances. Goal-Setting Effects: The study revealed that different goals could impact achievement and skill development differently, suggesting the need for future research on diversified goal-setting effects on teachers' perceived ICT self-efficacy and competencies. Trustworthiness Enhancement: The researchers suggested triangulating data sources, such as observations, to enhance the trustworthiness of the findings in future studies. Interpretation Complexity: Understanding the indirect effects of self-efficacy beliefs on ICT competencies through self-regulated learning strategies may have posed challenges in interpreting the results and implications accurately. Despite these obstacles, the researchers were able to navigate through them to provide valuable insights into the relationship between preservice teachers' self-efficacy beliefs, self-regulated learning strategies, and ICT competencies.

What was concluded from the article?

The article concluded that preservice teachers' beliefs in their capabilities of technology integration may not directly influence their technology use skills. Instead, these beliefs may influence technology use skills through self-regulated learning strategies, particularly goal-setting strategies. The study suggested that instructors and program coordinators could use instructional strategies or interventions to enhance preservice teachers' skills in setting their own learning goals, which could positively impact the transfer of ICT self-efficacy into actual technology use skills.

OPINION PERSONAL

Este estudio investiga la compleja relación entre la autoeficacia percibida de los docentes en la integración de tecnologías de la información y la comunicación (TIC) y su adquisición real de competencias en TIC, examinando el papel de las estrategias de aprendizaje autorregulado en línea (SRL). mediar en esta relación. A través de un análisis de 204 candidatos a docentes matriculados en un curso universitario de tecnología educativa, este estudio construye un modelo estructural para explicar las vías causales entre la autoeficacia en la integración de las TIC, las estrategias de SRL y las competencias en TIC. En particular, estos hallazgos resaltan que las creencias de autoeficacia de los futuros docentes con respecto a la integración de las TIC no influyen directamente en sus habilidades en TIC. Más bien, este impacto se logra indirectamente mediante la utilización de estrategias de SRL en línea, especialmente estrategias de establecimiento de objetivos. Esto subraya la importancia de fomentar estrategias de SRL, especialmente el establecimiento de objetivos, en los programas de formación docente para cerrar la brecha entre las creencias de autoeficacia y el dominio real de las TIC. Además, este estudio explica que las creencias de autoeficacia en la integración de las TIC muestran efectos positivos directos moderados en las estrategias de SRL combinadas y en las estrategias de SRL individuales, con la excepción de las estrategias de autoevaluación. Esto resalta la naturaleza multifacética del aprendizaje autodirigido y sugiere vías para intervenciones personalizadas para fortalecer las creencias de autoeficacia y las competencias SRL de los futuros maestros. Las implicaciones de estos hallazgos son enormes para las instituciones de formación de docentes encargadas de dotar a los futuros educadores de los conocimientos y habilidades necesarios en TIC. Al enfatizar el desarrollo de estrategias SRL, especialmente el establecimiento de objetivos, los programas de formación docente pueden aumentar la eficacia de los candidatos a docentes en la utilización de la tecnología en entornos educativos. Además, las intervenciones y estrategias de aprendizaje destinadas a desarrollar habilidades para establecer objetivos son prometedoras para facilitar la transferencia de la autoeficacia en TIC a habilidades reales de integración tecnológica. En conclusión, este estudio destaca la interrelación de las creencias de autoeficacia, las estrategias de aprendizaje autodirigido y las competencias en TIC entre los futuros docentes. Al explicar estas dinámicas, este informe proporciona información valiosa para las partes interesadas en la educación que buscan equipar a los educadores con las herramientas necesarias para prosperar en la era digital.
